Today, 56% of the global population lives in cities. This number is expected to increase to 68% by 2050. Our city governments are the stewards of valuable data, data systems and data policies. How might we better equip our cities to solve problems with data?

Application Process

Apply by August 6th and the City Incubator Team will review and provide feedback on your application if you desire. All final applications are due by August 31st. All applications require:

  • Completed application form: Application Form; PDF of Application Questions available here.
  • Data Innovation Canvas: All applications will require a Data Innovation Canvas to be completed and submitted. The Data Innovation Canvas is a data project scoping tool, a template is provided, no design is required. You can download a copy of the Data Innovation Canvas here.
  • Agree to participate for the full length of the six month program and acknowledge the time commitment : We expect program participants to engage in seven hours of programming and programming preparations per month in addition to making progress on launching their data innovation outside of City Incubator programming (recommend a minimum of 15 hours per month)
  • A letter of commitment from your organization’s leadership (Commissioner, Chief of Staff or Deputy Mayor)

If you have questions, please consult the FAQs at the bottom of this site. If after reading the FAQs you have additional questions, contact adrienne@thegovlab.org. All answers to questions sent via email will be added to the FAQ as a resource for all interested program participants.

Council of Mentors

Our Council of Mentors are experts from around the world in domains such as product management, performance management, data program development, data strategy, intrapreneurship and public sector problem solving. Mentors will be available to program participants throughout their six months with the City Incubator. This is our preliminary group of mentors; recruitment of additional mentors is taking place over the summer.
